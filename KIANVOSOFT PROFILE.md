**STARTUP COMPANY PROFILE**

# **1.0 About KianvoSoft**

KianvoSoft is a technology venture founded by Computer Engineering students of Mbeya University of Science and Technology (MUST), with a vision to build locally relevant digital solutions for East African markets. The venture brings together software engineering, technical training, and applied research under a single mission: to apply technology to the real problems faced by institutions, Health, businesses, and communities across the region.

Our work is organised around four interconnected pillars:

i.	Software Product Development \- designing and deploying software products that solve real problems for institutions, businesses, and communities, with a current focus on education technology, healthcare, and small-business digital tools.

ii.   Technical Training and Capacity Building \- delivering short courses, bootcamps, and coding camps in modern programming languages, frameworks, and emerging technologies for students and working professionals.

iii.  Applied Research in Artificial Intelligence \- conducting applied AI research with practical contributions to challenges in health, business, and the environment affecting the East African region.

iv.   Technology Consultancy and Community Outreach \- offering consultancy services to SMEs, NGOs, and institutions undertaking digital transformation, and running outreach programmes to inspire the next generation of African technologists.

# **2.0 Product and Research Portfolio**

Although KianvoSoft is in its early formal stages, the founders have already designed, built, and in several cases deployed a substantial portfolio of products and research outputs, spanning systems in active use with clients and platforms currently in development.

***2.1*** **Education Technology Platforms**

Kianvo Meet a video conferencing platform purpose-built to support online learning, collaboration, and professional meetings within East African institutions.

Kianvo Classroom \- a Learning Management System designed for East African universities, enabling course delivery, student engagement, assignments, and online assessment.

HELP (Health Education Learning Portal) \- an online learning platform focused on widening access to quality health education and continuing professional development.

***2.2*** **Business and Operations Systems**

Imforia \- a pharmacy management system designed to streamline stock, sales, and dispensing operations, currently in live use by pharmacies in Tanzania.

Mjenzi \- a point-of-sale and inventory management system tailored for hardware and construction-supply retailers.

ShopManagerPro \- a business management system tailored to the operational needs of small and medium-sized shops.

FleetLink \- a cargo transport management system that supports logistics operators in coordinating fleets, consignments, and deliveries.

IMS (Impact Management System) \- a platform that helps organisations plan, monitor, and report on the social and developmental impact of their work.

***2.3*** **Applied Research in Artificial Intelligence for Health**

Brain Stroke Lesion Detection from Medical Imaging \- an active research project applying artificial intelligence to support faster and more accessible stroke diagnosis in low-resource hospital settings.

Automated Malaria Detection through Computer Vision \- applied research on the automated identification of malaria parasites in blood samples using AI-powered image analysis, with the long-term goal of strengthening diagnostic capacity in rural health facilities.
