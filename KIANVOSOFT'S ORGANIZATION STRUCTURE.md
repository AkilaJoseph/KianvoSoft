**ORGANISATIONAL STRUCTURE AND GOVERNANCE**

# **1.0  Purpose of This Document**

This document defines the organisational structure, roles, decision-making framework, and operating rhythms of KianvoSoft. It serves three purposes: to provide clarity to the founders themselves on how the company is organised and how decisions are made; to give external parties — including the Centre for Innovation Technology Transfer (CITT), clients, partners, and prospective investors — a clear picture of how the venture is governed; and to lay a stable foundation on which the company can grow, hire, and operate as it scales.

# **2.0  Founding Philosophy**

KianvoSoft is built and run by three co-founders who hold equal authority in the leadership of the venture. The founders contribute across all areas of the business — engineering, research, training, business development, and operations — and operate as a single team rather than as separate departments working in isolation.

At the same time, the founders recognise that for the company to operate cleanly, scale beyond its founding stage, and be intelligible to outside parties, each major functional area requires a designated lead. The role of a lead is not to claim exclusive ownership of a domain, nor to set boundaries around who may contribute. The role of a lead is to serve as the accountable anchor for that domain: to convene work in the area, to act as the named external point of contact, to drive the day-to-day operating decisions within the lane, and to ensure that nothing falls through the cracks.

In short: all three founders do everything together; each founder leads one area.

# **4.0  Organisational Structure**

KianvoSoft is organised into three Directorates, each led by one of the co-founders. The Directorates are equal in standing. Each Directorate is the accountable home for the functional units listed under it, but the founders work jointly across all units as required.

**4.1  Directorate of Product & Engineering**

Led by Akila Body Joseph, Director of Product & Engineering.

Functional units and responsibilities:

•  Product Management — planning, prioritisation, and lifecycle management of all KianvoSoft software products.

•  Software Engineering — design, development, testing, and deployment of products including Kianvo Meet, Kianvo Classroom, HELP, Imforia, Mjenzi, ShopManagerPro, FleetLink, and IMS.

•  Quality Assurance — testing standards, release management, and post-release support.

•  Technical Infrastructure — cloud hosting, deployment pipelines, security, and developer tooling.

•  Customer Support (Technical) — handling client technical issues across deployed products.

**4.2  Directorate of Research & AI**

Led by Datius Rweyemamu Daud, Director of Research & AI.

Functional units and responsibilities:

•  Applied Research — active research projects in artificial intelligence for health, business, and the environment, including the brain stroke lesion detection and automated malaria detection initiatives.

•  Academic Partnerships — collaboration with MUST, with other universities, and with research institutions.

•  Publications and Knowledge Outputs — academic papers, technical reports, and dissemination of research findings.

•  Training and Capacity Building — design and delivery of short courses, bootcamps, and coding camps in programming, AI, and emerging technologies for students and professionals.

•  Research Ethics and Data Governance — ensuring research and data practices meet appropriate ethical and regulatory standards.

**4.3  Directorate of Business & Operations**

Led by Aneth Alphonce Seleli, Director of Business & Operations.

Functional units and responsibilities:

•  Business Development & Sales — client acquisition, partnerships, contracts, and revenue growth.

•  Finance and Administration — bookkeeping, budgeting, financial reporting, and day-to-day administration.

•  Human Resources — recruitment, internship coordination, staff welfare, and team development.

•  Marketing and Communications — brand identity, website, social media, and external communications.

•  Legal and Compliance — contracts, registrations, regulatory compliance, and intellectual property administration.

•  Institutional Relationships — engagement with CITT, MUST, government bodies, and other institutional partners.

# **5.0  External Representation**

KianvoSoft does not appoint a single permanent external spokesperson. Instead, the co-founder who represents the company externally is chosen on a per-engagement basis according to the nature of the engagement, by mutual agreement among the three Directors.

As a general guide:

•  Engagements concerning artificial intelligence, applied research, healthcare technology, training programmes, and academic collaboration are typically led by the Director of Research & AI.

•  Engagements concerning product demonstrations, client deployments, technical partnerships, and software architecture are typically led by the Director of Product & Engineering.

•  Engagements concerning business deals, partnerships, contracts, finance, investor relations, and institutional relationships are typically led by the Director of Business & Operations.

•  Engagements where audience or context calls for a particular voice, or that span multiple domains, may be led by two or all three Directors jointly.

For all binding legal and financial commitments, see Section 7.0 (Signatory Authority).

# **6.0  Governance and Decision-Making**

**6.1  Decision Categories**

Decisions in KianvoSoft fall into three categories:

i.	Routine Operating Decisions — day-to-day decisions within a Directorate's domain. The Director of that Directorate has the authority to decide, after appropriate consultation. Examples include task assignments, technical implementation choices, and routine communications.

ii.   Joint Operating Decisions — decisions that affect more than one Directorate or that shape the company at an operating level. These require the agreement of at least two of the three Directors. Examples include hiring, budgets above a defined threshold, taking on a new client, launching a new product, or signing a partnership.

iii.  Strategic and Founder Decisions — decisions that affect the ownership, direction, or fundamental structure of the company. These require the unanimous agreement of all three Directors. Examples include changes to the equity structure, taking on debt or equity investment, sale or merger of the company, addition or removal of a co-founder, and changes to this governance document.

**6.2  Meeting Rhythm**

•  Weekly Founders' Meeting — short, focused, reviewing progress across all three Directorates and unblocking each other for the week ahead.

•  Monthly Strategy Meeting — longer, reviewing finances, strategic direction, product roadmaps, research progress, and any pending Joint Operating Decisions.

•  Quarterly Review — full review of the previous quarter and planning for the next, including assessment of any Strategic Decisions on the horizon.

•  Ad-hoc Meetings — convened by any Director as needed for time-sensitive matters.

**6.3  Records and Accountability**

Decisions of categories (ii) and (iii) are recorded in writing in a shared decisions log, with a brief statement of the decision, the date, and the Directors who approved it. The Director of Business & Operations is the custodian of the decisions log.

# **7.0  Signatory Authority**

For matters that bind KianvoSoft legally or financially, the company adopts an "any two of three Directors" signatory rule. No single Director may, acting alone, commit the company to a binding agreement, a financial liability, or an obligation to a third party. Any two of the three Directors, acting together, are authorised to sign on behalf of KianvoSoft.

Documents requiring signature under this rule include, but are not limited to: contracts with clients and suppliers, partnership and collaboration agreements, leases and tenancy arrangements, opening and operating bank accounts, employment contracts, and instruments creating financial liability for the company.

Documents of formal institutional correspondence — including official letters to MUST, to CITT, and to government bodies — are signed by all three Directors as a matter of practice, except where the document itself specifies otherwise.

# **8.0  Dispute Resolution Among Founders**

Where the three Directors are unable to reach agreement on a Joint Operating Decision or a Strategic Decision, the following process applies, in order:

i.	The matter is held over for not less than seven (7) days, during which each Director prepares a written summary of their position and the reasoning behind it.

ii.   The Directors reconvene to consider the written positions and seek agreement.

iii.  Where agreement remains elusive, the matter is referred to the KianvoSoft Advisory Board (see Section 9.0) for non-binding guidance.

iv.   If after the Advisory Board's guidance no agreement is reached, the matter is decided by simple majority for Joint Operating Decisions, or held over indefinitely for Strategic Decisions until unanimity is achieved.

The Directors commit to engaging in good faith throughout this process and to placing the long-term interests of KianvoSoft above any short-term individual preference.

# **9.0  Advisory Board**

KianvoSoft establishes an Advisory Board of between two (2) and four (4) members. The Advisory Board is non-executive, holds no equity, and serves in an advisory capacity. Its role is to provide guidance, perspective, and constructive challenge to the Directors, and to support KianvoSoft's growth through their networks and expertise.

Advisory Board members are appointed by unanimous agreement of the three Directors, typically drawn from senior academic staff, established entrepreneurs in the Tanzanian innovation ecosystem, and domain experts relevant to KianvoSoft's product and research portfolio. Appointments are reviewed annually.

# **10.0  Growth and Future Hires**

As KianvoSoft grows, each Directorate will expand by adding team members — engineers, researchers, business and operations staff, interns, and contractors. New hires are positioned within the Directorate most aligned with their role, and report functionally to the Director of that Directorate.

Hiring decisions, including offer terms and titles, fall under Joint Operating Decisions and require the agreement of at least two of the three Directors. The Director of Business & Operations leads on contracts, onboarding, and HR administration regardless of which Directorate is hiring.
